Algobowl

It is coming up on Algobowl season for the algorithms class at Mines, and Dr. Mehta is working on putting together the teams.

He likes to have students work with people they know, as well as with people they don’t know.

In order to place students on teams that they know, he asked each student to submit to him one other person in the class that they would like to work with.

This year, he wants to honor each and every one of the student’s requests. That means if a student submitted a request, they will be placed on the same team as the person they requested.

For example, if Sean requested Mitch, Mitch requested Racheal, and Rachael requested Sean, all three of them would be placed on the same team.

If Dr. Mehta were to assign groups only based on the requests he received, how many groups would he have participating in Algobowl?

Note that this means there may not be the same number of students in each group.

The only information Dr. Mehta knows is the number of students in his class, the names of the students who submitted requests, and the student they requested.

The first line of the input file is the number of students in the class.

Each of the following lines contains two names separated by a “ – “ ; the first name is the student who submitted the request, and the second is who they requested.

The result should be an integer indicating the number of groups.
