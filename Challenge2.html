Old School Calculator

Imagine you have an old school calculator here is an image of one if needed

Each number in the display of the calculator has a possibility of 7 different lines that may or may not be lit up. For the digit 8 all 7 lines will light up, for the digit 7 only 4 will, etc.

For a given number what base between 2 and 16 would require the fewest number of lines to light up

(for bases above 10 let

A take 6 lines

B take 7 lines (you can’t tell the difference between B and 8 but that’s okay)

C take 4 lines

D take 6 lines (you can’t tell the difference between D and 0 but that’s okay)

E take 5 lines

F take 4 lines)

if we were to look at all the base 10 numbers between 0 through 1,000,000 (inclusive), determine which bases 2 through 16 would require the FEWEST lit-up lines if the number was displayed in that base, and sum up the total for each base.

Ties count toward all the bases in the tie. For each base you should print out Base [base number] = [total amount].
